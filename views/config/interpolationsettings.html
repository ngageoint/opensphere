Method

Also known as "shortest arc" or "orthodrome", a Geodesic is the shortest path between two points over the surface of the earth. This path follows a constantly-variable bearing and typically requires computer navigation equipment to follow.

Also known as "loxodrome" or "azimuth". This path, while not the shortest distance, follows a constant bearing over the surface of the earth. It is easier to navigate with a compass.
